## 1. AI Video Content Pipeline ("Cable" Project)

### Market Reality
- **AI slop is a $117M/year industry** (Guardian, Dec 2025) — 20%+ of videos shown to new YouTube users are AI-generated
- **Case study:** 22-year-old college dropout earning **$60K/month ($700K/year)** from AI-generated faceless YouTube channels (Fortune, Dec 2025)
- Multiple channels hitting 140M+ views with fully automated content

### Higgs Field Assessment
- **Legit and well-funded** — $1.3B valuation, $130M Series A (TechCrunch, Jan 2026)
- Founded by ex-Snap exec
- Cinema Studio: multi-shot, camera controls, lip sync, style consistency
- Sora 2 integration + sketch-to-video workflows
- **Free tier: 30 credits/day** — enough for 5-10 test videos/month
- Cloud API available for automation

### Cost Breakdown (per 1-minute video, 12 × 5s clips)
| Route | Cost | Quality |
|-------|------|---------|
| Budget (Wan 2.6) | ~$3 | Social media grade |
| Mid (Kling 2.6 Pro) | ~$4-8 | Cinematic |
| Premium (Veo 3.1) | ~$12 | Best lip sync/audio |

### Revenue Potential
- At $0.05 RPM and 100K views/video: **$5/video**
- At scale (10 videos/day, avg 50K views): **$750/month**
- Top performers earning $60K+/month with high volume + viral hits
- **Break-even:** ~60 videos at budget tier, faster at higher quality (better retention)

### Risks
- YouTube increasingly **labeling AI content** — could reduce reach
- Platform crackdowns possible (20% AI slop figure getting attention)
- Stock footage licensing varies by source — use Pexels/Pixabay (free) or budget for licensed
- **Competition is fierce** — low barrier means crowded market

### Verdict: 🟢 GO (as side project)
- Low upfront cost, free tiers available for MVP
- Real revenue proven by multiple creators
- Build with Higgs Field free tier → validate → scale with API
- **Timeline:** MVP in 3 days (as discussed), validate over 2 weeks

## 2. Discord Voice Transcription Bot

### Existing Competitors
- **Craig.chat** — Recording bot (audio only, no transcription)
- **SeaVoice** (seasalt.ai) — STT/TTS bot, speech-to-text in real-time
- **Scripty** — Live transcription bot
- **DiscMeet** — Meeting notes bot
- **Notesbot.io** — Records + writes summary
- **Mee6** — Recording behind paywall

### Legal Concerns
- **Discord ToS:** Bots can access voice channels with user permission, but recording requires explicit consent from all participants
- **GDPR (EU):** Recording conversations requires clear consent, right to deletion, data processing agreement
- **Wiretapping laws:** Many jurisdictions require all-party consent for recording
- **Risk level: HIGH** — especially for a public bot

### Monetization
- Freemium: X free hours/month, pay for more
- Premium features: AI analysis, action item extraction, searchable transcripts
- Target: gaming communities, study groups, remote teams using Discord
- **Market size:** ~200M Discord users, but niche use case

### Differentiation from Pascribe
- Existing bots do basic transcription — none do deep AI analysis + idea extraction
- Self-hosted/privacy-first angle is unique
- Group-focused (friend dynamics) vs business-focused (meetings)

### Verdict: 🟡 PROCEED WITH CAUTION
- Legal minefield for public bot — consent mechanisms needed
- Self-hosted version (like current Pascribe) is safer
- Could package as open-source tool rather than hosted service
- **Better as:** personal tool shared via repo, not a SaaS product

### Pascribe's Unique Position
- **Self-hosted** — no data leaves your server (privacy-first)
- **Discord-native** — built for friend groups, not boardrooms
- **Deep analysis** — not just transcript, but ideas, knowledge extraction, research suggestions
- **Automated pipeline** — VAD → transcription → AI analysis → Discord post
- **Cheap** — ~$1/5hrs via AssemblyAI vs $8-59/mo subscription

### Gap in Market

- Business tools are enterprise-focused, expensive, corporate
- Discord community wants lightweight, fun, social tool
- **OSS opportunity:** self-hosted with optional cloud analysis

### Monetization Options
1. **Open source + hosted cloud service** (freemium)
2. **One-time purchase** desktop app (Electron/Tauri)
3. **Discord bot marketplace** (Nitro-style subscription)

- Clear differentiation from enterprise meeting tools
- Self-hosted angle is hot in developer communities
